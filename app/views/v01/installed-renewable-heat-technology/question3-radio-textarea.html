How do I know if my new heating system has been commissioned?
Commissioning is when an installer tests the heating system. They do this to make sure the system works and meets industry standards. All radiators and underfloor heating pipes must be in place before this happens.

After passing the tests, the system is 'commissioned' as ready to provide heating and/or hot water. Â 

At this point, you get a Microgeneration Certification Scheme (MCS) certificate. This proves the system meets quality standards. Installers should include the MCS certificate in their handover pack.
